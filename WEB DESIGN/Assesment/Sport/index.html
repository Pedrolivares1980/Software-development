Heroes del Silencio, the band

Héroes del Silencio (sometimes abbreviated to Héroes or by their initials HDS) was a Spanish rock
band based in Zaragoza, initially formed by Juan Valdivia (lead guitar) and Enrique Bunbury (vocals and rhythm guitar),
and completed by Joaquín Cardiel (bass) and Pedro Andreu (drums), in the mid-1980s.

They began to get together to play in pubs in 1984, but it was not until 1985 that the group took
its definitive form. Their musical discoverer was the guitarist of the Spanish band Ole-Ole.

In 1987 they signed their first record contract and the following year they released their first album,
a 4-song LP. In 1988 they released "El mar no cesa", obtaining an unexpected success, ending their fame
in 1990 with "Senderos de traición". The band from Zaragoza sells half a million records and performs very
successful concerts in Germany, Italy, Belgium, Holland and obviously Spain.

Then comes "El espíritu del vino" (1993), where there is a much more rocker change in their music due to the
production of Phil Manzanera, throwing off their fans, but gaining the respect of the critics. As soon as the
album was released, they embarked on the "El Camino del Exceso" tour, which would take them through Europe
and America with a total of 134 concerts, and incorporated the Mexican guitarist Alan Boguslavsky as second guitarist.

In 1995 they released their fourth studio album, "Avalancha", and toured Europe for more than four months.
The relationship between the members of the band was not going well, even before recording "Avalancha",
so they released their live album, called "Para Siempre", as a farewell.
Before concluding the tour "Tour Avalancha" and in a press conference held in Lima, Héroes del Silencio
announced their temporary separation.

The success of Héroes del Silencio did not cease in spite of their separation, and that is why in the years
after their breakup they continued to release albums (compilations, rarities, etc.).

In 2005, the band's first DVD was released along with a CD called "El ruido y la furia" (The noise and the fury),
which compiles the best versions of their classic songs.

In 2007, after 11 years of separation, the band reunites again to perform 10 concerts in stadiums in Argentina,
Mexico, Guatemala and Spain, among other countries. This tour was recorded in video and audio and from it
"Héroes del Silencio Tour 2007" is edited, in Cd, Cd+Dvd and special box version.

In November 2011 the Dvd "Live in Germany" is released; it is a concert that the band gave in Germany in 1993, on
the occasion of the presentation of the emblematic album "El espíritu del vino". The material was released in Cd,
Cd+Dvd and Dvd+Vinyl.

In 2015 Héroes del Silencio reissued the album "Senderos de Traición", 25 years after its original release.

In January 2021 the album "El Espíritu del vino" was re-released 28 years after its release, becoming No. 1
in sales in Spain. On April 23, the band premieres a documentary on their artistic career on Netflix.
